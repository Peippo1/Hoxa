# Hoxa Product Brief

## Overview

Hoxa is a lifestyle-led fitness product that helps beginner to intermediate users train toward meaningful goals without forcing them into a single discipline. It blends running, strength, mobility, balance, and recovery into a supportive adaptive plan that fits around work, energy, and life schedule.

## Problem

Most fitness products push users into one narrow lane:

- running apps optimise for mileage but underplay strength and mobility
- workout apps offer libraries but not coherent progression toward a goal
- training plans often break down when real life interrupts the schedule
- recovery and balance are treated like optional extras

Users who want to prepare for a race, build strength, stay mobile, and remain consistent end up stitching together multiple tools with no clear weekly system.

## Target User

Hoxa is built for people who:

- are new to structured training or returning after time away
- want credible guidance without an intimidating tone
- train across home, gym, and outdoor running environments
- care about consistency, energy, and realistic scheduling
- may use Apple Watch, Apple Health, Garmin, Google Calendar, or Apple Calendar over time

## Example Goals

- run a 10K at a target pace
- complete a first half marathon
- improve balance and mobility
- build chest and upper body strength
- train consistently around work and life schedule
- combine 2 runs, 2 strength sessions, and 1 mobility session per week

## Core Value Proposition

Hoxa gives users a realistic adaptive training calendar that helps them pursue specific goals while balancing strength, running, mobility, balance, and recovery in one coherent system.

## Differentiator

The key differentiator is the adaptive calendar. Instead of only recommending sessions, Hoxa reshapes the week around:

- selected goal and timeframe
- available training days
- preferred environments such as home, gym, and outdoors
- missed sessions
- accumulated fatigue and recent volume
- supporting work such as mobility, balance, and recovery

## Product Pillars

### 1. Plan

- onboarding and goal setup
- preferred training split
- weekly calendar view
- adaptive recommendations when life changes
- future calendar syncing

### 2. Train

- session details and structured workouts
- running sessions, gym sessions, home workouts, mobility, balance, and recovery
- manual workout logging
- future wearable-driven workout history

### 3. Progress

- consistency trends
- weekly and monthly training load
- milestone progress toward goals
- streaks, totals, and modality balance

### 4. Social

- follow friends
- see recent completed sessions
- give props
- lightweight accountability without creating pressure or comparison-heavy feeds

## Brand Direction

Hoxa should feel:

- modern
- calm
- premium
- editorial
- breathable
- elegant
- fitness-forward without being macho

Visual inspiration comes from warm hospitality and lifestyle brands rather than hardcore performance dashboards.

- `#C0E1D2`

## UX Principles

- supportive over punitive
- smart but easy to understand
- spacious over cluttered
- calm confidence over performance anxiety
- recovery and mobility made visible and valuable

## MVP Scope

### In scope

- onboarding
- goal selection
- adaptive weekly training calendar
- workout library
- session detail views
- manual workout logging
- progress dashboard
- lightweight follows and props
- Apple Health and calendar integration-ready architecture

### Out of scope for first build

- full wearable sync implementation
- complex coaching AI
- live messaging
- advanced nutrition tracking
- deep route planning
- large-scale community features

## Platform Strategy

- `iOS`: primary consumer experience
- `Web`: planning, dashboard, and social companion
- `Backend`: future intelligence, integrations, sync, and social infrastructure

## Success Criteria For MVP

- a new user can onboard and choose a goal in under 3 minutes
- the weekly plan clearly explains what to do and why
- users can view, complete, and log sessions easily
- progress feels motivating without becoming noisy
- the architecture can support future Apple Health, Garmin, and calendar integrations without large rewrites
