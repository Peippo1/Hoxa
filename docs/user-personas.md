# Hoxa User Personas

## Why These Personas Matter

Hoxa is designed for hybrid fitness users who need structure but do not want a punishing or hyper-technical product. These personas keep the MVP grounded in believable product decisions.

## Persona 1: Maya, First Half Marathon Builder

- Age: 31
- Training level: beginner to lower intermediate
- Context: works full-time, wants to build endurance without losing strength
- Devices: Apple Watch, iPhone
- Constraints: weekday training windows are short and inconsistent

### Goals

- complete a first half marathon
- keep two weekly strength sessions
- avoid injury and burnout

### Needs From Hoxa

- a weekly plan that explains session purpose
- easy rescheduling when work gets in the way
- mobility reminders that feel essential, not optional
- a calm progress view that shows consistency

## Persona 2: Elliot, Busy General Fitness Hybrid

- Age: 28
- Training level: beginner
- Context: wants a realistic structure that mixes home workouts, gym sessions, and easy runs
- Devices: none required yet
- Constraints: schedule changes often and motivation fluctuates

### Goals

- train consistently around work
- build upper body and chest strength
- feel fitter without following separate apps and plans

### Needs From Hoxa

- simple onboarding
- adaptable training mix
- short session options
- encouragement without pressure

## Persona 3: Harper, Mobility And Balance Restarter

- Age: 37
- Training level: returning user
- Context: has done cardio before but wants better movement quality and physical confidence
- Devices: Garmin watch
- Constraints: intimidated by performance-heavy fitness products

### Goals

- improve mobility and balance
- keep a low-pressure weekly routine
- gradually add strength

### Needs From Hoxa

- mobility and balance treated as first-class training
- welcoming tone
- low-friction session logging
- milestone moments that celebrate consistency

## MVP Implications

- onboarding must ask about goals, schedule, equipment, and training preferences
- the plan must support mixed modality weeks
- mobility and recovery must be visible in the calendar and progress views
- social must stay lightweight and privacy-aware
